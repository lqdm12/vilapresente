What this place is

Vila Presente is a working permaculture farm in the Atlantic Forest. Not a resort. A place where the banana trees were planted three years ago, where the walls are still being built from earth, and where the food on your plate was in the ground this morning.

We run small immersions, maximum 8 people, for those who want to understand how an agroforest actually works, what natural building feels like from the inside

This is not a performance of sustainability. It's the thing itself.

Atlantic Forest

One of Earth's most biodiverse biomes. Less than 12% remains. You'll be inside it — not looking at it from a viewpoint.

Syntropic Agroforestry

Banana, avocado, coffee, native fruits — planted in layers that feed each other. The forest feeds itself, and then it feeds you.

Natural Building

Tires, earth, straw, bamboo. Ancient techniques actively in use. You won't just see finished walls — you'll build them.

From the forest to the table

You'll eat things you can't
find on any menu.

Brazil's Atlantic Forest holds thousands of edible plants that never made it to agriculture. Every meal at Vila Presente is built around what the farm produces that day. Nothing imported. Nothing that traveled further than you can see from the table.

Ask about the food →
Ora-pro-nóbis Protein-rich leaf. Grows on the fence. Goes in everything from omelets to pasta.
Taioba Giant edible leaf related to taro. Nutty, rich, used in traditional Minas Gerais cooking.
Capuchinha Nasturtium. Flowers, leaves, seeds all edible. Peppery, bright, beautiful on the plate.
Beldroega Purslane. Highest omega-3 of any vegetable. Grows wild between every other plant.
Serralha Wild sow thistle. Sautéed with garlic and olive oil. Disappears off the plate.
Caruru Amaranth. Ancient grain. Goes into the most iconic Bahian dishes in Brazil.

A typical week

Learn by doing

1–2
Tire Wall Building

Foundation to finish. Learn ramming, leveling, and why tires work in tropical climates.

3
Agroforestry Design

Plant a syntropic row. Walk the existing system. Understand why each species is where it is.

4
Brazilian Kitchen

Cook with what we harvest. Farofa, fermentation, wood-fire cooking with PANCs.

5
Natural Finishes

Clay plaster and lime. Hands on the wall. Finish what the first two days started.

6
Community & Culture

Visit neighbors. Learn essential Portuguese. Basket weaving with banana fiber.

7
Integration

What do you take home? A session to map what you learned to your own life and land.

Schedule flows with weather and group energy — this is real life, not a resort program.
