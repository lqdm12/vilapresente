Learn by Doing: A Typical Week

Day 1-2: Tire Wall Bioconstruction

Learn rammed earth techniques, thermal mass principles, and help shape the foundation of our next guest cabin. No experience needed.

Day 3-4: Syntropic Agroforestry

Map, plant, and prune food forests using Ernst Götsch's methods. Understand succession layers, biomass cycling, and natural fertility.

Day 5: Water & Soil Systems

Build swales, keyline irrigation, and compost toilets. Learn how to read water flow and restore degraded land.

Day 6-7: Harvest, Preserve & Celebrate

Process what you've grown, ferment, cook together, and close with a community fire circle. Leave with skills, not souvenirs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior permaculture experience?

No. We teach from the ground up. Beginners learn alongside experienced practitioners. Curiosity and willingness to work are the only requirements.

What's the physical demand like?

Moderate to high. You'll lift earth, dig, carry timber, and work outdoors in varying weather. Pace is adjusted to group ability, but this is active, not passive.

Is travel/visa included?

No. You arrange your own flights to São Paulo or Rio. We'll help coordinate ground transport to the retreat site and assist with Brazilian visa documentation.

Can I extend my stay or return later?

Yes. Founders get priority booking for 2026 programs. Many guests return for volunteer stays or longer immersions once they experience the rhythm of Vila Raiz.
